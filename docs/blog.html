Ghana's Geospatial Society Calls for Technology Adoption to Improve Governance

At the Second Annual General Meeting of the Ghana Geospatial Society in Accra, President Michael Nyoagbe emphasized the crucial role of geospatial technology in resource access and improved governance. Key highlights include:

  • Advocacy for policies supporting geospatial data sharing and open access
  • Investment in training programs for next-generation geospatial professionals
  • Applications in flood mapping, transportation planning, and agriculture
  • Integration of GIS, remote sensing, and location-based analytics across industries

The conference theme was "Geospatial Excellence for Digital Growth: Fostering Innovation and Collaboration to Unlock Ghana's Potential" :cite[2].

Source: Ghana News Agency (November 2024)

Global Geospatial Analytics Market to Reach $55.75 Billion by 2029

Posted

Ghana Parliament Approves Gold Board Bill with Geospatial Implications

Posted

The new Ghana Gold Board will regulate gold sector activities, requiring improved geospatial tracking of mining operations. The board replaces PMMC with enhanced responsibilities for gold acquisition, sale, and export monitoring :cite[5].
